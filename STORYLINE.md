# LODESTAR_FREEDOM AND JUSTICE — Narrative Canon

**Purpose:** This document is the single source of truth for story, character, and historical intent. Any new mission, dialogue, UI text, or mechanic that touches narrative **must align with this file**. If a change contradicts it, update this document first — never drift silently.

**Version:** 1.0  
**Setting:** Accra, Gold Coast — late 1947 through 28 February 1948  
**Genre:** Tactical resistance drama (squad-command), not alternate history.

---

## 1. Core Premise (Non-Negotiable)

The player commands **Sergeant Adjetey**, **Corporal Attipoe**, and **Private Odartey Lamptey** — ex-servicemen of the Gold Coast — as they prepare for the peaceful petition march to **Christiansborg Castle**.

The struggle is for **self-government and dignity**, not revenge fantasy. Violence in gameplay represents **colonial enforcement and desperate defense**, not glorification of armed revolution in this period.

**Winning the campaign means:** gathering evidence, protecting organizers, sustaining the march, and documenting truth — **not** rewriting history so the three martyrs survive or the British are militarily defeated.

## 2. Historical Anchors (Do Not Break)

These events are **fixed in the canon timeline**. Missions may happen *around* them; they may not erase or replace them.

| Date / Period | Event | Canon status |
|---------------|--------|--------------|
| WWII aftermath | Gold Coast veterans promised benefits; payments withheld or delayed | **True** |
| 1947 | UGCC and nationalist networks organize; Nkrumah rises as key voice | **True** |
| Late 1947 | Ex-servicemen's Union agitates for owed compensation | **True** |
| Pre–28 Feb 1948 | Petition drafted; peaceful march planned to Governor at Christiansborg | **True** |
| **28 Feb 1948** | Unarmed veterans march; confrontation at **Christiansborg Crossroads** | **True — sacred beat** |
| **28 Feb 1948** | **Adjetey, Attipoe, Lamptey** shot and killed by colonial police under **Superintendent Colin Imray** | **True — do not retcon survival** |
| **28 Feb 1948** | Petition to **Governor Gerald Creasy** blocked at Christiansborg Crossroads | **True** |
| Late 1947 | **Nii Kwabena Bonne III** boycott movement fuels nationalist organizing in Accra | **True context** |
| March 1948 | Accra riots; Nkrumah arrested; Watson Commission follows | **True aftermath** |
| Long arc | Path toward independence (1957) | **Implied epilogue only — not playable in POC** |

### Historical sensitivity rules

1. **The three ex-servicemen are martyrs**, not comic action heroes. Honor their real names and sacrifice.
2. **Mission 10** may let the player succeed at *witnessing, extracting evidence, or saving other marchers* — **not** at preventing the Crossroads shootings entirely.
3. **Nkrumah** is a political strategist who urges **peaceful, disciplined** protest. He is not a battlefield commander ordering assassinations.
4. **Colonial forces** are oppressive structures and armed enforcers — avoid cartoon racism; depict institutional violence and fear.
5. **Civilian protesters** are unarmed. Player failure to protect them is narrative weight, not a joke.

## 4. Thematic Pillars (Story Must Serve These)

1. **Debt and dignity** — veterans owed benefits; the march is also about recognition.
2. **Peaceful defiance** — the petition is the primary weapon until state violence forces crisis.
3. **Truth as ammunition** — intel, ledgers, witnesses, and smuggled copies matter as much as shooting.
4. **Trust under pressure** — Asare arc mirrors colonial divide-and-rule vs. nationalist unity.
5. **Sacrifice with consequence** — Crossroads deaths **ignite** the riots; they are not erased by player skill.

## 6. Mission-by-Mission Story Locks

Each entry: **Purpose**, **Must happen**, **Must not happen**.

### M1 — Ex-Servicemen's Oath
- **Purpose:** Establish veteran grievance and squad identity.
- **Must happen:** Ledgers prove withheld benefits; Asare first helpful contact.
- **Must not happen:** Open war on Castle; Nkrumah field commander fantasy.

### M2 — Kaneshie Lines
- **Purpose:** UGCC network visible; escort = protection of peaceful organizing.
- **Must happen:** Organizers reach safe house; forged curfew pass from Asare.
- **Must not happen:** Organizers armed or turned into soldiers.

### M3 — Radio Hill
- **Purpose:** Tactical strike on communications; Lamptey's role.
- **Must happen:** Charges planted; hold while arming; colonial patrol intel from Asare proves accurate.
- **Must not happen:** Total destruction of Accra infrastructure; mass civilian casualties.

### M4 — Checkpoint James Town
- **Purpose:** Open smuggling route; seed Asare unreliability (red herring).
- **Must happen:** Checkpoint cleared; pamphlet intel about peaceful Castle petition.
- **Must not happen:** Asare exposed as traitor definitively — only doubt.

### M5 — The Governor's Courier
- **Purpose:** Intelligence coup; deepen Asare suspicion.
- **Must happen:** Courier captured alive; partial orders; **missing page** after Asare handoff.
- **Must not happen:** Courier killed as success state; full orders revealing Crossroads date prematurely.

### M6 — Ambush at Makola ⭐ Betrayal beat
- **Purpose:** Emotional low; only Asare knew the route.
- **Must happen:** Ambush; Attipoe wounded; colonial radio: *"Wrong corridor — false corridor."*
- **Must not happen:** Asare confirmed traitor in UI; permanent Attipoe death in gameplay before M10.

### M7 — Shadow the Clerk
- **Purpose:** Reveal double-agent performance.
- **Must happen:** Player tails Asare; witnesses fake map handoff; Nkrumah line: *"Some lies protect true marchers."*
- **Must not happen:** Asare becomes playable squad member; assassination of colonial sergeant as required win.

### M8 — Hold Christ Church
- **Purpose:** Petition read publicly; scale of movement.
- **Must happen:** Hold square; protect speakers; wanted poster for Asare.
- **Must not happen:** Petition burned or replaced with violent manifesto.

### M9 — The March Column
- **Purpose:** Ex-servicemen march unarmed; world watching.
- **Must happen:** Column reaches Crossroads approach; Asare smoke signal; falsified gate counts.
- **Must not happen:** Squad opens fire on marchers; column armed with heavy weapons.

### M10 — Christiansborg Crossroads ⭐ Climax
- **Purpose:** Document truth; honor martyrs; launch historical consequence.
- **Must happen:** Witness nodes; Asare radio reveal; petition copy secured; epilogue names martyrs.
- **Must not happen:** Player prevents the three deaths; British military routed; "happy ending" independence on same day.
- **Win condition (story):** Evidence out, survivors extracted, **martyrdom acknowledged in text**.

## 8. Dialogue & UI Writing Rules

1. **Briefings:** Max ~2 sentences gameplay + 1 historical tone line. Sign-off: *"— Nkrumah"* or *"Cmdr. Nkrumah:"*
2. **Intel popups:** Past tense or discovered-document voice; cite concrete detail (Castle, petition, benefits, dates).
3. **No modern slang:** Avoid memes, Twitter voice, anachronistic US military jargon ("ROE," "kIA" in dialogue — HUD abbreviations OK).
4. **Ghana / Gold Coast naming:** Use **Gold Coast** in 1948 colonial-period text; **Ghana** only in epilogue or player-facing meta (title screen OK).
5. **Enemy barks:** Minimal or absent in POC; if added — orders to disperse, maintain order, not racial slurs.
6. **Victory / defeat:** Victory advances **independence meter** (movement momentum), not personal glory.

## 15. One-Paragraph Elevator Pitch (Locked)

*LODESTAR_FREEDOM AND JUSTICE* puts you in command of three ex-servicemen who fought for Britain and came home owed nothing. As Accra prepares for a peaceful petition march to Christiansborg Castle, you gather evidence, protect organizers, and command your squad through colonial checkpoints — while clerk **Kofi Asare** may be saving you or selling you out. The tactical fight is for truth and momentum; the historical end is fixed: **28 February 1948** at Christiansborg Crossroads, where sacrifice sparks a nation's path to freedom.

**Phase IV (The Powder Keg):** After Crossroads, **Kojo** commands a four-person cell — **Araba** enforces, **Kofi** grows from rookie to fighter, **Kwesi** handles intel without combat — guided between missions by mentor **Yaw**, whose grandfather marched with the martyrs.
